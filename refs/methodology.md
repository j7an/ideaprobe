# Validation Methodology

## Why Structured Validation Matters

The most common failure mode for solo builders and small teams is building before validating. You spend 4 weeks on an MVP, launch it, and discover nobody wants it — or that 15 competitors already own the space. IdeaProbe exists to prevent this.

Structured validation takes hours, not weeks. It replaces gut feelings with data. It catches STOP signals before you invest time.

## The 90-Day MVP Decision Cycle

IdeaProbe is optimized for builders working in 90-day cycles:
- **Weeks 1-2:** Validate the idea (this is what IdeaProbe does)
- **Weeks 3-6:** Build the MVP
- **Weeks 7-12:** Launch, get users, measure signals
- **Day 90:** Go/no-go decision on continuing

The faster you validate, the more build time you have. IdeaProbe compresses weeks of research into a structured workflow.

## Core Principles

1. **Cite data, not vibes.** Every score must reference a specific finding — a search trend, a Reddit post, a competitor's pricing page. If you can't cite it, you can't score it.

2. **Be honest about weak signals.** Don't oversell moderate results as strong. A "moderate demand" signal is useful information — it tells the builder to dig deeper, not to start coding.

3. **When the verdict is STOP, say so clearly.** The hardest part of validation is accepting a negative result. A clear STOP saves months of wasted effort. Frame it constructively: "This idea has weak demand. Here's why. Move to your next idea."

4. **Triangulate across dimensions.** No single data point is a verdict. Strong demand with no willingness to pay is still risky. Great founder fit with zero demand is still a STOP.

5. **Absence of signal IS a signal.** If nobody is complaining about a problem online, that usually means there's no market — not that you've found a hidden gem. The exception: brand-new technology creating problems that didn't exist before.

6. **Data quality matters.** A single Reddit post is an anecdote. 50 posts over 2 years describing the same pain is a signal. Always note how much evidence supports each finding.

## How Skills Compose

IdeaProbe skills form a natural workflow, but each works standalone:

- **No idea yet:** `idea-generation` → `idea-comparison` → `idea-validation` → `mvp-scoping`
- **Has one idea:** `idea-validation` → `mvp-scoping`
- **Has many ideas:** `idea-comparison` → `idea-validation` (on winner) → `mvp-scoping`
- **Needs depth on one dimension:** `market-research`, `competitor-analysis`, or `community-sentiment` standalone
- **Profile management:** `founder-profile` anytime

## What IdeaProbe Is NOT

- **Not a business plan generator.** It validates whether an idea is worth pursuing — it doesn't produce a 50-page business plan.
- **Not a comprehensive market report.** It gathers enough data to make a go/no-go decision. For deep market analysis, hire a research firm.
- **Not a substitute for talking to real customers.** IdeaProbe finds signals in public data. The best validation comes from direct conversations with potential customers. IdeaProbe tells you whether those conversations are worth having.
- **Not financial advice.** Scores and verdicts are informational tools. The builder makes the final decision.
